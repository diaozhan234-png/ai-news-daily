Sam Altman responds to ‘incendiary’ New Yorker article after attack on his home
OpenAI CEO Sam Altman published a blog post on Friday evening responding to both an apparent attack on his home and an in-depth New Yorker profile raising questions about his trustworthiness. Early Friday morning, someone allegedly threw a Molotov cocktail at Altman’s San Francisco home. No one was hurt in the incident, and a suspect was later arrested at OpenAI headquarters, where he was threatening to burn down the building, according to the SF Police Department . While the police have not identified the suspect publicly, Altman noted that the incident came a few days after “an incendiary article” was published about him. He said someone had suggested that the article’s publication “at a time of great anxiety about AI” could make things “more dangerous” for him. “I brushed it aside,” Altman said. “Now I am awake in the middle of the night and pissed, and thinking that I have underestimated the power of words and narratives.” The article in question was a lengthy investigative piece written by Ronan Farrow (who won a Pulitzer for reporting that revealed many of the sexual abuse allegations around Harvey Weinstein) and Andrew Marantz (who’s written extensively about technology and politics). Farrow and Marantz said that during interviews with more than 100 people who have knowledge of Altman’s business conduct, most described Altman as someone with “a relentless will to power that, even among industrialists who put their names on spaceships, sets him apart.” Echoing other journalists who have profiled Altman , Farrow and Marantz suggested that many sources raised questions about his trustworthiness, with one anonymous board member saying he combines “a strong desire to please people, to be liked in any given interaction” with “a sociopathic lack of concern for the consequences that may come from deceiving someone.” In his response, Altman said that looking back, he can identify “a lot of things I’m proud of and a bunch of mistakes.” Among the mistakes, he said, is a tendency towards “being conflict-averse,” which he said has “caused great pain for me and OpenAI.” “I am not proud of handling myself badly in a conflict with our previous board that led to a huge mess for the company,” Altman said, presumably referring to his removal and rapid reinstatement as OpenAI CEO back in 2023. “I have made many other mistakes throughout the insane trajectory of OpenAI; I am a flawed person in the center of an exceptionally complex situation, trying to get a little better each year, always working for the mission.” He added, “I am sorry to people I’ve hurt and wish I had learned more faster.” Altman also acknowledged that there seems to be “so much Shakespearean drama between the companies in our field,” which he attributed to a “‘ring of power’ dynamic” that “makes people do crazy things.” Of course, the correct way to deal with the ring of power is to destroy it, so Altman added, “I don’t mean that [artificial general intelligence] is the ring itself, but instead the totalizing philosophy of ‘being the one to control AGI.’” His proposed solution is “to orient towards sharing the technology with people broadly, and for no one to have the ring.” Altman concluded by saying that he welcomes “good-faith criticism and debate,” while reiterating his belief that “technological progress can make the future unbelievably good, for your family and mine.” “While we have that debate, we should de-escalate the rhetoric and tactics and try to have fewer explosions in fewer homes, figuratively and literally,” he said.

Sam Altman在家中遭到袭击后回应了《纽约客》的“煽动性”文章
OpenAI首席执行官Sam Altman周五晚上发表了一篇博客文章，回应了对他家的明显攻击以及《纽约客》对他的可信度提出的质疑。周五凌晨，据称有人向奥尔特曼旧金山的家中扔了一杯燃烧瓶鸡尾酒。据旧金山警察局称，事件中没有人受伤，一名嫌疑人后来在OpenAI总部被捕，当时他威胁要烧毁大楼。虽然警方尚未公开确认嫌疑人的身份，但奥特曼指出，这起事件发生在一篇关于他的“煽动性文章”发表几天后。他说，有人暗示，这篇文章的发表“在人们对人工智能非常焦虑的时候”可能会让事情对他来说“更危险”。“我把它放在一边，”奥特曼说。“现在我半夜醒来，很生气，认为我低估了文字和叙事的力量。”这篇文章是罗南·法罗（Ronan Farrow，因揭露了哈维·韦恩斯坦周围的许多性虐待指控而获得普利策奖）和安德鲁·马兰茨（Andrew Marantz，他撰写了大量关于技术和政治的文章）撰写的一篇长篇调查文章。Farrow和Marantz表示，在对100多名了解奥特曼商业行为的人的采访中，大多数人都将奥特曼描述为“有着无情的权力意志，即使是在那些把自己的名字写在宇宙飞船上的实业家中，这也使他与众不同”。Farrow和Malantz与其他报道奥特曼的记者一样，暗示许多消息来源对他的可信度提出了质疑，一位匿名董事会成员表示，他将“强烈的取悦他人的欲望，在任何特定的互动中都能被喜欢”与“对欺骗他人可能带来的后果缺乏社会关怀”相结合。奥特曼在回应中说，回首往事，他可以发现“很多让我感到自豪的事情”。他说，这些错误中有一种倾向是“厌恶冲突”，他说这“给我和OpenAI带来了巨大的痛苦”“我并不为自己在与前任董事会的冲突中处理不当而感到自豪，这场冲突导致了公司的巨大混乱，”Altman说，他可能指的是2023年他被免职并迅速恢复OpenAI首席执行官的职位。“在OpenAI的疯狂发展过程中，我还犯了很多其他错误；我是一个有缺陷的人，处于异常复杂的情况中心，每年都在努力变得更好，总是为任务而努力。”他补充道，“我对我伤害过的人感到抱歉，希望我能更快地学习。”奥特曼还承认，“我们这个领域的公司之间似乎有太多莎士比亚戏剧”，他将其归因于“权力的循环”，“让人们做出疯狂的事情。”当然，处理权力环的正确方法是摧毁它，所以奥特曼补充道，戒指本身，而是‘成为控制AGI的人’的总体哲学。”他提出的解决方案是“面向广泛地与人们共享技术，而不是让任何人拥有戒指。”奥特曼最后表示，他欢迎“善意的批评和辩论”同时重申他的信念，即“技术进步可以为你的家人和我的家人创造令人难以置信的美好未来。”他说：“在我们进行这场辩论的同时，我们应该缓和言辞和策略，尽量减少在更少的家庭中发生的爆炸，无论是比喻上还是字面上。”。
