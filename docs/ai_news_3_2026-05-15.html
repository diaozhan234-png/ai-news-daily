What the jury will actually decide in the case of Elon Musk vs. Sam Altman
Nine California jurors are now deliberating over the future of OpenAI, the world-leading artificial intelligence lab. While the trial exploring Elon Musk’s case against OpenAI’s other cofounders and Microsoft has covered territory ranging from the breakup of the founders in 2018 to Altman’s firing and rehiring in 2023, the jurors will be considering a set of fairly narrow questions. OpenAI has also made three arguments in its defense that the jury will weigh: If Musk wins out, it could mean the end of OpenAI as a for-profit company, but it’s not entirely clear what will result. Next week, the judge will begin a set of new hearings where lawyers from both sides will debate what the consequences of a verdict in favor of the plaintiffs might be. That process could be rendered moot by a negative verdict, however. Musk’s attorneys say the defendants clearly understood that Musk wanted to support a non-profit that would ensure the benefits of AI to the world, and prevent it from being controlled by any one organization. In particular, they say a $10 billion investment from Microsoft in 2023 into OpenAI’s for-profit affiliate—the first to happen after the statute of limitations—was the event that turned Musk’s concern into conviction. That deal, Musk’s lawyers say, was different from previous investments and led to OpenAI’s investors being enriched by the company’s commercial products, at the expense of the charitable mission of AI safety that Musk promoted. OpenAI’s attorneys have asked every witness to describe specific restrictions put on Musk’s donations, and none have, including his financial adviser Jared Birchall, his chief of staff Sam Teller, or his special adviser Shivon Zilis. They say everyone involved agreed that private fundraising would be required to achieve its goals, and note that Musk himself attempted to launch an OpenAI-affiliated for-profit he would personally control, and later to merge OpenAI into his company Tesla. They also note the organization’s other donors haven’t said their charitable trust was violated. Importantly, a forensic accountant hired by OpenAI testified that all of Musk's donations had been used by OpenAI well before the key date of August 5, 2021. That is evidence that Musk's donations were already used for their purpose well before he brought his lawsuit, invalidating any charitable trust that may have existed. Mainly, they insist that the for-profit affiliate that conducts most of OpenAI's actual activity continues to fulfill the organization's mission, and has generated nearly $200 billion in equity value to support the non-profit foundation. Notably, Sam Altman argued that providing ChatGPT for free helps fulfill the mission of sharing the benefits of AI with the world. The plaintiffs point to the multibillion-dollar valuations of stakes held by OpenAI founders like Brockman and Ilya Sutskever, as well as Microsoft itself, as a sign that Musk's donations were ultimately used for personal benefit, as opposed to supporting the mission of the charity. They argue that the work at OpenAI's for-profit was commercially focused, while the foundation itself was left essentially dormant, without full-time employees, and, ultimately, not even in control of the for-profit. OpenAI says all of Musk's contributions were used by the foundation by 2020, and that equity distributions came well after he left the organization in 2018. Even beforehand, evidence shows the key players agreed that being able to compensate researchers with stock was key to developing AGI, the hypothetical form of AI capable of performing any intellectual task a human can. OpenAI executives maintain that the for-profit's work meaningfully advanced the foundation's mission, including safety activities. They say the non-profit board continues to control the for-profit, and instituted new governance controls following "the blip," when Altman was fired by OpenAI's non-profit board in 2023 for lack of candor and then rehired just days later. Musk's case focused on the events of the blip, when Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella, whose company depended on OpenAI's tech, was personally involved with helping to bring Altman back and creating a new board to govern OpenAI. They note that Microsoft executives wondered if their commercial agreement might conflict with the non-profit's goals, and suggest that Microsoft's commercial priorities led OpenAI away from its mission. They've focused attention on a clause in Microsoft's agreement with OpenAI that gave Microsoft veto rights over major corporate decisions at OpenAI. Microsoft's witnesses have insisted that the company's executives didn't know of any specific conditions on Musk's donations despite extensive due diligence, and never vetoed any decision by OpenAI. They note that the company's investments and compute power allowed OpenAI to achieve its biggest triumphs. Musk has suggested that his skepticism of his cofounders grew over time, until in the fall of 2022 he finally decided they had betrayed him when he found out about Microsoft's plans for a new $10 billion investment that took place in 2023. He wouldn't file his lawsuit until mid-2024. OpenAI's attorneys argue that the terms of that deal were spelled out in a term sheet for a previous fundraising round in 2018, which Musk received and his advisers reviewed, but Musk said he didn't read in detail. They also note numerous blog posts and other communications from over the years that show Musk could have known what OpenAI was doing well before he brought them to court, including tweets where Musk criticized the company years before the suit. Zilis, Musk's adviser, even voted to approve these transactions as a member of the OpenAI board.

九名加州陪审员现在正在审议世界领先的人工智能实验室OpenAI的未来。虽然对埃隆·马斯克（Elon Musk）针对OpenAI其他联合创始人和微软的案件的审判涵盖了从2018年创始人分手到2023年奥特曼（Altman）被解雇和重新聘用的各个领域，但陪审员将考虑一系列相当狭隘的问题。OpenAI在辩护中还提出了三个论点，陪审团将对此进行权衡：如果马斯克获胜，这可能意味着OpenAI作为一家营利性公司的终结，但目前尚不完全清楚结果会是什么。下周，法官将开始一系列新的听证会，双方律师将辩论有利于原告的裁决的后果。然而，这一过程可能会因负面裁决而变得毫无意义。马斯克的律师表示，被告清楚地理解马斯克希望支持一个非营利组织，以确保人工智能为世界带来利益，并防止其被任何一个组织控制。特别是，他们表示，微软在2023年向OpenAI的营利性附属公司投资了100亿美元，这是诉讼时效后的第一次投资，这一事件使马斯克的担忧变成了定罪。马斯克的律师表示，这笔交易与之前的投资不同，导致OpenAI的投资者因该公司的商业产品而致富，而牺牲了马斯克所倡导的人工智能安全慈善使命。OpenAI的律师要求每一位证人描述对马斯克捐款的具体限制，但没有人这样做，包括他的财务顾问Jared Birchall、他的幕僚长Sam Teller或他的特别顾问Shivon Zilis。他们说，所有参与其中的人都同意，需要私人融资才能实现其目标，并指出马斯克本人试图推出一个他个人控制的OpenAI附属营利组织，后来又将OpenAI合并到他的公司特斯拉。他们还指出，该组织的其他捐助者没有表示他们的慈善信托受到了侵犯。重要的是，OpenAI聘请的一名法务会计师作证说，马斯克的所有捐款早在2021年8月5日的关键日期之前就被OpenAI使用了。这证明马斯克的捐款早在他提起诉讼之前就已经用于他们的目的，使任何可能存在的慈善信托无效。主要是，他们坚持认为，负责OpenAI大部分实际活动的营利性附属机构继续履行该组织的使命，并产生了近2000亿美元的股权价值来支持该非营利基金会。值得注意的是，Sam Altman认为，免费提供ChatGPT有助于实现与世界分享人工智能好处的使命。原告指出，Brockman和Ilya Sutskever等OpenAI创始人以及微软本身所持股份的数十亿美元估值表明，马斯克的捐款最终用于个人利益，而不是支持慈善机构的使命。他们认为，OpenAI的营利性工作主要集中在商业上，而基金会本身基本上处于休眠状态，没有全职员工，最终甚至无法控制营利性企业。OpenAI表示，到2020年，马斯克的所有捐款都被基金会使用，股权分配在他2018年离开该组织后很好地进行了。甚至在之前，有证据表明，关键参与者一致认为，能够用股票补偿研究人员是开发AGI的关键，AGI是一种能够执行人类可以执行的任何智力任务的人工智能的假设形式。OpenAI的高管们坚称，该营利组织的工作有意义地推进了基金会的使命，包括安全活动。他们说，非营利委员会继续控制营利组织，并在2023年Altman因缺乏坦诚而被OpenAI的非营利委员会解雇，几天后又被重新聘用后，在“短暂的”之后制定了新的治理控制措施。马斯克的案件集中在短暂的事件上，当时微软首席执行官萨蒂亚·纳德拉（Satya Nadella）的公司依赖于OpenAI的技术，他亲自参与帮助奥特曼回归，并创建了一个新的董事会来管理OpenAI。他们指出，微软高管想知道他们的商业协议是否会与非营利组织的目标相冲突，并表示微软的商业优先事项导致OpenAI偏离了其使命。他们将注意力集中在微软与OpenAI协议中的一项条款上，该条款赋予微软对OpenAI重大企业决策的否决权。微软的证人坚称，尽管进行了广泛的尽职调查，但该公司的高管并不知道马斯克捐款的任何具体情况，也从未否决过OpenAI的任何决定。他们指出，该公司的投资和计算能力使OpenAI取得了最大的成功。马斯克表示，随着时间的推移，他对联合创始人的怀疑越来越大，直到2022年秋天，当他发现微软计划在2023年进行100亿美元的新投资时，他终于决定他们背叛了他。他直到2024年年中才提起诉讼。OpenAI的律师辩称，该交易的条款已在2018年上一轮融资的条款清单中详细说明，马斯克收到了该条款清单，他的顾问也对其进行了审查，但马斯克表示他没有详细阅读。他们还注意到，多年来的许多博客文章和其他通信表明，马斯克在将他们告上法庭之前可能已经知道OpenAI做得很好，包括马斯克在诉讼前几年批评该公司的推文。马斯克的顾问Zilis甚至以OpenAI董事会成员的身份投票批准了这些交易。
