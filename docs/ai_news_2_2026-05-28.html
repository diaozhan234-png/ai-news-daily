Why Google’s AI can’t spell Google (or anything else)
How many Ps are in Google? According to Google, there are two. There’s also is also “exactly 1 ‘r’ in the word ‘poop’,” Google’s AI Overview says, as well as two ‘d’s in the word journalism, yet spelled it: j-o-u-r-n-a-d-i-s-m. Google did at least identify that there is one P in the last name of the U.S. president, but spelled it as t-r-p-u-m. You didn’t need to be a prophet to predict that Google’s AI-forward Search overhaul was going to go over poorly. We’ve done this before. The first time Google added AI Overviews to Search, the feature ended up citing satirical posts from The Onion and Reddit , advising people to eat rocks and put glue on their pizza. This time around, as Google doubles down on its commitment to make generative AI the centerpiece of its 29-year-old flagship product, it’s not surprising to see it stumble. “Counting within words has been a known challenge for LLMs, and we’re working to fix this particular issue,” Google told TechCrunch in an emailed statement. These basic spelling errors may seem familiar. LLMs, the kind of artificial intelligence that powers chatbots and other text-generators, are not built to understand spelling. It’s been a running joke for years that whenever a company unveils a new AI model, you should ask it how many ‘r’s are in the word strawberry . These AI models — which can code an app in seconds, or solve problems that have stumped mathematicians for decades — are about as good as a kindergartener at spelling. Google’s AI overview woes reach beyond silly spelling mistakes though. Google already patched an issue from last week in which searching the word “ disregard ” would yield what looked like a dictionary definition of the word, only the definition was shown as, “Understood. Let me know whenever you have a new prompt or question!” But these spelling errors have remained amusing because they’re so difficult to quash. As researchers have previously explained when we’ve asked about these spelling conundrums, AI doesn’t perceive sentences as units of language made up of words and letters. Many LLMs are built on transformers models, which break down text into tokens, which can be full words, syllables, or letters, depending on the model. Instead of “reading” like a human would, the AI converts the text into numerical representations of itself, which are then contextualized to help the AI come up with a logical response. “LLMs are based on this transformer architecture, which notably is not actually reading text. What happens when you input a prompt is that it’s translated into an encoding,” Matthew Guzdial, an AI researcher and assistant professor at the University of Alberta, told TechCrunch . “When it sees the word ‘the,’ it has this one encoding of what ‘the’ means, but it does not know about ‘T,’ ‘H,’ ‘E.’” The token-based architecture that powers LLMs like Google’s AI overview is inherently limiting, and researchers haven’t been optimistic that they can solve the spelling problem. “It’s kind of hard to get around the question of what exactly a ‘word’ should be for a language model, and even if we got human experts to agree on a perfect token vocabulary, models would probably still find it useful to ‘chunk’ things even further,” Sheridan Feucht, a PhD student studying large language model interpretability at Northeastern University, told TechCrunch . “My guess would be that there’s no such thing as a perfect tokenizer due to this kind of fuzziness.” This isn’t necessarily an urgent problem on researchers’ minds, since the utility of LLMs doesn’t come in their capacity to spell. But these blatant failures help us remember that AI is not perfect, even if it may sometimes seem like an all-knowing power beyond our comprehension. 为什么谷歌的人工智能不能拼写谷歌（或其他任何东西）
Google中有多少个P？根据谷歌的说法，有两个。谷歌的AI Overview表示，“poop”这个词中也有一个“r”，新闻这个词中还有两个“d”，但拼写为：j-o-u-r-n-a-d-i-s-m。谷歌至少确定了美国总统的姓氏中有一个P，但拼写成t-r-P-u-m。你不需要是先知就可以预测谷歌的人工智能搜索改革会失败。我们以前做过。谷歌首次将AI Overviews添加到搜索中时，该功能最终引用了洋葱和Reddit的讽刺帖子，建议人们吃石头，在披萨上涂胶水。这一次，随着谷歌加倍努力，将生成性人工智能作为其29年旗舰产品的核心，看到它步履蹒跚也就不足为奇了。谷歌在一封电子邮件声明中告诉TechCrunch：“字数统计对LLM来说是一个已知的挑战，我们正在努力解决这个特殊问题。”。这些基本的拼写错误可能看起来很熟悉。LLM是一种为聊天机器人和其他文本生成器提供动力的人工智能，它不是为理解拼写而构建的。多年来，每当一家公司推出新的人工智能模型时，你都应该问它草莓这个词中有多少个r，这一直是一个笑话。这些人工智能模型可以在几秒钟内编写一个应用程序，或者解决困扰数学家几十年的问题，在拼写方面和幼儿园的孩子一样好。不过，谷歌的人工智能概述问题不仅限于愚蠢的拼写错误。谷歌已经修复了上周的一个问题，在这个问题中，搜索“无视”一词会得到一个看起来像字典定义的单词，只有定义显示为“理解”。如果你有新的提示或问题，请随时告诉我！“但这些拼写错误仍然很有趣，因为它们很难消除。正如研究人员之前在询问这些拼写难题时所解释的那样，人工智能不会将句子视为由单词和字母组成的语言单位。许多LLM都是基于transformers模型构建的，该模型将文本分解为标记，标记可以是完整的单词、音节或字母，具体取决于模型。人工智能没有像人类那样“阅读”，而是将文本转换为自身的数字表示，然后将其上下文化，以帮助人工智能做出逻辑反应。阿尔伯塔大学人工智能研究员兼助理教授Matthew Guzdial告诉TechCrunch：“LLM基于这种转换器架构，它实际上并不是在阅读文本。当你输入提示时，它会被翻译成编码。”。“当它看到‘the’这个词时，它对‘the’的含义进行了编码，但它不知道‘T’、‘H’、‘E’。”为谷歌的人工智能概述等LLM提供支持的基于令牌的架构本质上是有限的，研究人员对他们能解决拼写问题并不乐观。东北大学研究大型语言模型可解释性的博士生Sheridan Feucht告诉TechCrunch：“对于语言模型来说，‘单词’到底应该是什么这个问题很难回避，即使我们让人类专家就一个完美的表征词汇达成一致，模型可能仍然会发现进一步‘分块’是有用的。”。“我的猜测是，由于这种模糊性，没有完美的标记器。”这对研究人员来说不一定是一个紧迫的问题，因为LLM的实用性并不在于他们的拼写能力。但这些公然的失败帮助我们记住，人工智能并不完美，即使它有时看起来像是一种我们无法理解的无所不知的力量。我们不能盲目信任人工智能输出，而不仔细检查其准确性。当您通过我们文章中的链接购买时，我们可能会获得一小笔佣金。这并不影响我们的编辑独立性。Amanda Silberling是TechCrunch的高级作家，报道技术和文化的交叉点。她还为Polygon、MTV、《肯扬评论》、NPR和《商业内幕》等出版物撰稿。她与科幻作家伊莎贝尔·J·金共同主持了关于网络文化的播客《哇，如果真的》。在加入TechCrunch之前，她曾担任基层组织者、博物馆教育者和电影节协调员。她拥有宾夕法尼亚大学英语学士学位，并在老挝担任普林斯顿亚洲研究员。您可以通过电子邮件联系或验证Amanda的联系方式amanda@techcrunch.com或通过Signal上的@amanda.100加密消息发送。
