Claude, OpenClaw and the new reality: AI agents are here — and so is the chaos
The age of agentic AI is upon us — whether we like it or not. What started with an innocent question-answer banter with ChatGPT back in 2022 has become an existential debate on job security and the rise of the machines. More recently, fears of reaching artificial general intelligence (AGI) have become more real with the advent of powerful autonomous agents like Claude Cowork and OpenClaw . Having played with these tools for some time, here is a comparison. First, we have OpenClaw (formerly known as Moltbot and Clawdbot). Surpassing 150,000 GitHub stars in days, OpenClaw is already being deployed on local machines with deep system access. This is like a robot “maid” (Irona for Richie Rich fans, for instance) that you give the keys to your house. It’s supposed to clean it, and you give it the necessary autonomy to take actions and manage your belongings (files and data) as it pleases. The whole purpose is to perform the task at hand — inbox triaging, auto-replies, content curation, travel planning, and more. Next we have Google’s Antigravity , a coding agent with an IDE that accelerates the path from prompt to production. You can interactively create complete application projects and modify specific details over individual prompts. This is like having a junior developer that can not only code, but build, test, integrate, and fix issues. In the realworld, this is like hiring an electrician: They are really good at a specific job and you only need to give them access to a specific item (your electric junction box). Finally, we have the mighty Claude. The release of Anthropic's Cowork, which featured AI agents for automating legal tasks like contract review and NDA triage, caused a sharp sell-off in legal-tech and software-as-a-service (SaaS) stocks (referred to as the SaaSpocalypse ). Claude has anyway been the go-to chatbot; now with Cowork, it has domain knowledge for specific industries like legal and finance. This is like hiring an accountant. They know the domain inside-out and can complete taxes and manage invoices. Users provide specific access to highly-sensitive financial details. The key to making these tools more impactful is giving them more power, but that increases the risk of misuse . Users must trust providers like Anthorpic and Google to ensure that agent prompts will not cause harm, leak data, or provide unfair (illegal) advantage to certain vendors. OpenClaw is open-source, which complicates things, as there is no central governing authority. While these technological advancements are amazing and meant for the greater good, all it takes is one or two adverse events to cause panic. Imagine the agentic electrician frying all your house circuits by connecting the wrong wire. In an agent scenario, this could be injecting incorrect code, breaking down a bigger system or adding hidden flaws that may not be immediately evident. Cowork could miss major saving opportunities when doing a user's taxes; on the flip side, it could include illegal writeoffs. Claude can do unimaginable damage when it has more control and authority. But in the middle of this chaos, there is an opportunity to really take advantage. With the right guardrails in place, agents can focus on specific actions and avoid making random, unaccounted-for decisions. Principles of responsible AI — accountability, transparency, reproducibility, security, privacy — are extremely important. Logging agent steps and human confirmation are absolutely critical. Also, when agents deal with so many diverse systems, it's important they speak the same language. Ontology becomes very important so that events can be tracked, monitored, and accounted for. A shared domain-specific ontology can define a “code of conduct." These ethics can help control the chaos. When tied together with a shared trust and distributed identity framework, we can build systems that enable agents to do truly useful work. When done right, an agentic ecosystem can greatly offload the human “cognitive load” and enable our workforce to perform high-value tasks. Humans will benefit when agents handle the mundane. Claude、OpenClaw和新现实：人工智能代理在这里——混乱也是如此
不管我们喜欢与否，代理人工智能的时代已经到来。早在2022年，与ChatGPT的一场无害的问答玩笑就已经演变成了一场关于工作保障和机器崛起的存在主义辩论。最近，随着Claude Cowork和OpenClaw等强大的自主智能体的出现，人们对实现通用人工智能（AGI）的担忧变得更加真实。在使用这些工具一段时间后，这里有一个比较。首先，我们有OpenClaw（以前称为Moltbot和Clawdbot）。OpenClaw在几天内就超过了150000个GitHub star，已经部署在具有深度系统访问权限的本地机器上。这就像一个机器人“女仆”（例如，里奇粉丝的Irona），你把房子的钥匙交给她。它应该清理它，你给它必要的自主权，让它随心所欲地采取行动和管理你的物品（文件和数据）。整个目的是执行手头的任务——收件箱分类、自动回复、内容管理、旅行计划等等。接下来，我们有谷歌的Antigravity，一个带有IDE的编码代理，可以加速从提示到生产的路径。您可以交互式地创建完整的应用程序项目，并在单个提示下修改特定的详细信息。这就像让一个初级开发人员不仅可以编码，还可以构建、测试、集成和修复问题。在现实世界中，这就像雇佣一名电工：他们真的很擅长一项特定的工作，你只需要让他们接触到一个特定的项目（你的电接线盒）。最后，我们有强大的克劳德。Anthropic的Cowork以人工智能代理为特色，用于自动化合同审查和NDA分类等法律任务，导致法律技术和软件即服务（SaaS）股票（称为SaaSpocalypse）大幅抛售。不管怎样，克劳德一直是聊天机器人的首选；现在有了Cowork，它拥有法律和金融等特定行业的领域知识。这就像雇佣一名会计师。他们对域名了如指掌，可以完成税务和管理发票。用户提供对高度敏感的财务细节的特定访问权限。使这些工具更具影响力的关键是赋予它们更多的权力，但这会增加误用的风险。用户必须信任Anthorpic和谷歌等提供商，以确保代理提示不会造成伤害、泄露数据或向某些供应商提供不公平（非法）优势。OpenClaw是开源的，这使事情变得复杂，因为没有中央管理机构。虽然这些技术进步是惊人的，并且是为了更大的利益，但只需要一两个不利事件就会引起恐慌。想象一下，代理电工通过连接错误的电线来烧毁你家的所有电路。在代理场景中，这可能是注入不正确的代码、破坏更大的系统或添加可能不会立即显现的隐藏缺陷。在为用户纳税时，联合办公可能会错过主要的储蓄机会；另一方面，这可能包括非法减记。当克劳德拥有更多的控制权和权威时，它会造成难以想象的伤害。但在这场混乱中，有一个真正利用的机会。有了正确的护栏，代理人可以专注于具体的行动，避免做出随机、不负责任的决定。负责任的人工智能原则——问责制、透明度、可重复性、安全性、隐私性——极其重要。日志代理步骤和人工确认是绝对关键的。此外，当代理处理如此多的不同系统时，他们使用相同的语言很重要。本体变得非常重要，这样可以跟踪、监视和解释事件。共享的特定领域本体可以定义“行为准则”。这些道德规范可以帮助控制混乱。当与共享的信任和分布式身份框架结合在一起时，我们可以构建使代理能够做真正有用的工作的系统。如果做得对，代理生态系统可以大大减轻人类的“认知负荷”，使我们的员工能够执行高价值的任务。当代理处理日常事务时，人类将受益。Dattaraj Rao是Persistent systems的创新和研发架构师。欢迎来到VentureBeat社区！我们的客座计划是技术专家分享见解，并对人工智能、数据基础设施、网络安全和其他塑造企业未来的尖端技术进行中立、非既定的深入研究的地方。从我们的客座计划中阅读更多信息——如果你有兴趣写一篇自己的文章，请查看我们的指南！
