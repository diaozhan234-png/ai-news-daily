Ferrari is using IBM’s AI to create F1 superfans
Two years ago, IBM realized there was one glaring omission in its roster of sports partnerships: Formula One. Formula One has become one of the world’s most popular sports, especially in the U.S., where Netflix’s “Drive to Survive” documented the working lives of F1 drivers and turned them into mainstream celebrities. The tech-centric sport has also become a hot ticket for tech companies like AWS, Oracle, and Anthropic, which partner with teams for sponsorship visibility and to provide data analytics and AI tools that can deliver a competitive edge. So when IBM went looking for its next major sports partnership, it’s no wonder the company picked F1 and one of its most iconic teams, Scuderia Ferrari HP . “They’re the winningest team in history,” Kameryn Stanhouse, IBM’s Vice President of Sports and Entertainment Partnerships, told TechCrunch. At the heart of this partnership, however, is what has led other teams to start working with tech giants: access to more sophisticated tech solutions that can help them make the most of, especially, artificial intelligence. In fact, one of the best parts of sports, Stanhouse said, is how much data is available and can be used to help people get comfortable with AI. “They actually see how it serves them,” she said of how AI is used in sports storytelling. The IBM-Ferrari partnership centers on that idea of storytelling, enhancing fan engagement by overhauling the technology powering the Ferrari fan app. To help with this, Ferrari hired Stefano Pallard in the newly titled role “head of fan development,” who said the challenge the team wanted to tackle was not just reaching fans, but “making each of them feel like we know them.” “That starts with taking the data we get from the track and turning it into content that is easy to follow and engaging,” he told TechCrunch. Teams process millions of data points per second during each race, capturing every movement of the driver and the car. Turning this into content that fans can engage with is just one way that advanced enterprise AI can help businesses better interact with their consumers. Among the 11 teams, Ferrari is one of the few (alongside the likes of McLaren and Williams) to have a standalone fan app strategy rather than lean on social media or the official F1 platforms instead, showing how the sport is slowly starting to capitalize on its growing global fandom. Some of the changes to the Ferrari app were simple, like offering it in Italian. Even though Ferrari is an Italian company and many of its fans are Italian, their fan app was not available in Italian until the IBM partnership. Stanhouse said the old Ferrari fan app was a place where people went to find race details and then leave. This new app has games where fans can play with others in the app, new AI-written race summaries, more behind-the-scenes stories about the team and the drivers, a place to make predictions, and an AI companion for fans to ask questions. “There are two drivers, but did you know it takes 24 people working simultaneously in two seconds to change a tire?” Stanhouse said, adding that storytelling helps fans feel closer to the team. Unlike other sports apps IBM has built, Stanhouse said the Ferrari app’s main focus is storytelling because it wants fans to stay engaged with it all year long, rather than for a few weeks a year, as with tournaments like the Masters. Engagement data for the app has been trending upward since IBM came into the scene, Stanhouse said, citing a 62% increase in engagement over race weekends as an example. Pallard said the team then uses AI to analyze engagement signals in the app, such as which content people like to read and the sentiment of the messages fans send.

法拉利正利用IBM的人工智能打造F1超级跑车
两年前，IBM意识到其体育合作伙伴名单中有一个明显的遗漏：一级方程式赛车。一级方程式赛车已经成为世界上最受欢迎的运动之一，尤其是在美国，Netflix的《Drive to Survive》记录了F1车手的工作生活，并将他们变成了主流名人。这项以技术为中心的运动也成为AWS、Oracle和Anthropic等科技公司的热门门票，这些公司与团队合作，提供赞助可见性，并提供可以提供竞争优势的数据分析和人工智能工具。因此，当IBM开始寻找下一个主要的体育合作伙伴时，难怪该公司选择了F1及其最具标志性的车队之一法拉利车队。IBM体育和娱乐合作伙伴副总裁Kameryn Stanhouse告诉TechCrunch：“他们是历史上获胜最多的团队。”。然而，这种合作关系的核心是促使其他团队开始与科技巨头合作的原因：获得更复杂的技术解决方案，帮助他们充分利用人工智能。斯坦豪斯说，事实上，体育最好的部分之一是有多少数据可用，可以用来帮助人们适应人工智能。“他们实际上看到了人工智能是如何为他们服务的，”她谈到了人工智能在体育叙事中的应用。IBM与法拉利的合作关系围绕着讲故事的理念，通过彻底改革法拉利粉丝应用程序的技术来提高粉丝的参与度。为了帮助实现这一点，法拉利聘请了Stefano Pallard担任新任命的“粉丝开发主管”一职，他说车队想要解决的挑战不仅仅是接触粉丝，而是“让他们每个人都觉得我们了解他们”。“这始于将我们从赛道上获得的数据转化为易于遵循和吸引人的内容，”他告诉TechCrunch。车队在每场比赛中每秒处理数百万个数据点，捕捉车手和赛车的每一个动作。将其转化为粉丝可以参与的内容只是高级企业人工智能帮助企业更好地与消费者互动的一种方式。在这11支车队中，法拉利是为数不多的（与迈凯轮和威廉姆斯等车队一样）拥有独立粉丝应用策略而不是依赖社交媒体或官方F1平台的车队之一，这表明这项运动正慢慢开始利用其日益增长的全球粉丝群。法拉利应用程序的一些更改很简单，比如用意大利语提供。尽管法拉利是一家意大利公司，其许多粉丝都是意大利人，但在与IBM合作之前，他们的粉丝应用程序还没有意大利语版本。斯坦豪斯说，旧的法拉利粉丝应用程序是人们去寻找比赛细节然后离开的地方。这款新应用程序有粉丝可以在应用程序中与其他人一起玩的游戏、新的人工智能编写的比赛摘要、更多关于车队和车手的幕后故事、一个进行预测的地方，以及一个供粉丝提问的人工智能伴侣。斯坦豪斯说：“有两个司机，但你知道吗，在两秒钟内需要24个人同时工作才能换轮胎？”他补充说，讲故事有助于球迷感觉更接近球队。斯坦豪斯表示，与IBM开发的其他体育应用程序不同，法拉利应用程序的主要重点是讲故事，因为它希望粉丝全年都能参与其中，而不是像大师赛这样的锦标赛那样，一年只参与几周。Stanhouse表示，自IBM进入该领域以来，该应用程序的参与度数据一直呈上升趋势，例如比赛周末的参与度增加了62%。Pallard说，该团队随后使用人工智能来分析应用程序中的参与信号，例如人们喜欢阅读哪些内容以及粉丝发送的信息的情绪。
