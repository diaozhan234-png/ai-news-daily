Coders are refusing to work without AI — and that could come back to bite them
In 2026, you cannot pry AI coding tools out of developers’ vise grip, researchers have discovered. But while AI is undoubtedly helping coders produce code faster, it may not be producing better code, other researchers warn. And that could cause problems down the road for them. Specifically, in February 2026, respected AI research lab METR published a surprising revelation: Most developers won’t work, even on a limited number of tasks, without AI anymore. METR had hoped to provide an update to some groundbreaking research published a few months earlier, in 2025, on AI coding productivity. In it, researchers measured how much time open source developers took to do tasks by hand versus with AI. While developers in that study reported that AI was making them more productive, they were shocked to learn it actually slowed them down. Sure, it generated code faster, but then they spent extra time finding and fixing errors, steering the AI and waiting on it to complete tasks. When METR set out to repeat the experiment to measure advances in AI and coder proficiency, they couldn’t. Devs weren’t willing to participate “because they do not wish to work without AI” even just for the study, the researchers confessed. Instead, METR published a survey in May that allowed technical employees to self-report their AI productivity gains. Not surprisingly, they perceived that AI made them twice as valuable to their organizations. But recent headlines about the wild expense of so-called tokenmaxxing , coupled with a smattering of recent research, make such self-perceptions dubious. Tokenmaxxing, or using the number of tokens a person uses as a proxy for productivity with AI, has been the trend of 2026 so far. And it may already be over. Amazon shut down its internal token-tracking leaderboard called Kirorank after employees were gaming it by using AI agents excessively, and running up costs, the Financial Times reported this week. The employees proved that AI use does not automatically translate to increased productivity. Uber blew through its 2026 AI budget within the first four months of the year, The Information reported. COO Andrew Macdonald recently said on a podcast that such spending hadn’t led to a measurable increase in projects or productivity. AI-generated code also doesn’t necessarily reduce ongoing code maintenance needs and may even increase it, programmer and author James Shore elegantly argued in a blog post that went viral on Hacker News. “You write code twice as quick now? Better hope you’ve halved your maintenance costs,” he wrote. “Otherwise, you’re screwed. You’re trading a temporary speed boost for permanent indenture.” There’s other evidence that AI can increase code maintenance woes.

程序员拒绝在没有人工智能的情况下工作，这可能会反过来伤害他们
研究人员发现，2026年，你无法将人工智能编码工具从开发人员的虎钳中撬出来。但是，其他研究人员警告说，尽管人工智能无疑可以帮助程序员更快地生成代码，但它可能不会生成更好的代码。这可能会在未来给他们带来问题。具体来说，2026年2月，受人尊敬的人工智能研究实验室METR发布了一个令人惊讶的发现：没有人工智能，大多数开发人员将无法工作，即使是在有限的任务上。METR曾希望为几个月前，即2025年发表的一些关于人工智能编码生产力的突破性研究提供最新进展。在该研究中，研究人员测量了开源开发人员手工完成任务与使用人工智能完成任务的时间。虽然该研究中的开发人员报告说人工智能使他们更有效率，但他们惊讶地发现，人工智能实际上减缓了他们的工作速度。当然，它生成代码的速度更快，但随后他们花费了额外的时间来查找和修复错误，引导人工智能并等待它完成任务。当METR开始重复实验以衡量人工智能和编码器熟练程度的进步时，他们做不到。研究人员承认，开发人员不愿意参与“因为他们不希望在没有人工智能的情况下工作”，即使只是为了这项研究。相反，METR在5月份发布了一项调查，允许技术员工自我报告他们的人工智能生产力提高。毫不奇怪，他们认为人工智能使他们对组织的价值提高了一倍。但最近关于所谓的tokenmaxxing的疯狂开支的头条新闻，再加上最近的一些研究，让这种自我认知变得可疑。Tokenmaxxing，即使用一个人使用的代币数量作为人工智能生产力的代理，是2026年迄今为止的趋势。而且可能已经结束了。据英国《金融时报》本周报道，亚马逊关闭了名为Kirorank的内部代币跟踪排行榜，原因是员工过度使用人工智能代理，并增加了成本。员工们证明，人工智能的使用不会自动转化为生产力的提高。据《信息报》报道，优步在今年前四个月内就花光了2026年的人工智能预算。首席运营官Andrew Macdonald最近在播客中表示，这种支出并没有导致项目或生产力的显著提高。程序员兼作家James Shore在Hacker News上疯传的一篇博客文章中优雅地指出，人工智能生成的代码也不一定会减少持续的代码维护需求，甚至可能会增加代码维护需求。他写道：“你现在写代码的速度快了一倍？最好希望你的维护成本减半。”。“否则，你就完蛋了。你正在用暂时的速度提升换取永久的契约。”还有其他证据表明，人工智能会增加代码维护的麻烦。
