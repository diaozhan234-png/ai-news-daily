Google, Accel India accelerator choses 5 startups and none are ‘AI wrappers’
Many artificial intelligence startup ideas are still little more than superficial “wrappers” built on top of existing models. But as the AI model makers add more features, investors are wary of startups that could become so easily unnecessary. Case in point: when reviewing more 4,000 applications for the joint AI accelerator for India startups run by Google and venture firm Accel, “wrapper” ideas dominated. But none of them were among the five startups for the latest cohort, Accel partner Prayank Swaroop told TechCrunch (pictured above). Announced in November, the AI-focused Atoms program by Google and Accel aims to back early-stage startups building AI products linked to India. Startups selected for the latest cohort will receive up to $2 million in funding from Accel and Google’s AI Futures Fund , along with up to $350,000 in cloud and AI compute credits from Google, the firms said. Roughly 70% of the rejected applications were “wrappers” — startups that layered AI features such as chatbots on top of existing software but “were not reimagining new workflows using AI,” Swaroop said. Many of the remaining applications that were denied, Swaroop said, fell into crowded categories such as marketing automation and AI recruitment tools, areas where investors saw little novelty. Startups in those sectors often struggle to differentiate themselves, he said. This isn’t, perhaps, surprising. This year’s program received nearly four times the applications than previous Accel’s Atoms cohorts — with many first-time founders. India’s growing AI ecosystem remains largely focused on enterprise applications and Swaroop said the applications reflected that. About 62% of the submissions focused on productivity tools and another 13% on software development and coding, meaning around three-quarters of the applications were enterprise software ideas rather than consumer products. (Swaroop had hoped to see more ideas for healthcare and education.) Jonathan Silber, co-founder and director of Google’s AI Futures Fund, said the five startups selected aligned closely with areas where Google expects AI to see deeper real-world adoption. The program does not require startups to use Google’s models exclusively, Silber said, noting that many companies combine multiple models depending on the workflow. The goal, he said, is to gather feedback from startups on how Google’s models perform in real-world applications. Insights from those startups can then be fed back to Google DeepMind teams to help improve future models, creating what Silber described as a “flywheel” between startup experimentation and AI development. “If a company is using an alternative model, that means Google has work to do to build the best model in the market,” he told TechCrunch. 谷歌，Accel India加速器选择了5家初创公司，没有一家是“AI包装商”
许多人工智能初创公司的想法仍然只是建立在现有模型之上的肤浅的“包装器”。但随着人工智能模型制造商添加更多功能，投资者对那些很容易变得不必要的初创公司持谨慎态度。一个恰当的例子是：在审查谷歌和风险投资公司Accel为印度初创公司运营的联合人工智能加速器的4000多份申请时，“包装器”的想法占主导地位。但Accel合伙人Prayank Swaroop告诉TechCrunch（如上图所示），他们都不在最新一批创业公司的五家初创公司之列。谷歌和Accel于11月宣布了以人工智能为重点的Atoms计划，旨在支持与印度相关的早期初创公司开发人工智能产品。两家公司表示，入选最新队列的初创公司将获得Accel和谷歌人工智能期货基金高达200万美元的资金，以及谷歌高达35万美元的云和人工智能计算信贷。Swaroop说，大约70%的被拒绝的应用程序是“包装器”——初创公司在现有软件的基础上分层了聊天机器人等人工智能功能，但“没有使用人工智能重新构想新的工作流程”。Swaroop说，许多被拒绝的剩余申请属于拥挤的类别，如营销自动化和人工智能招聘工具，投资者在这些领域几乎看不到什么新鲜事。他说，这些领域的初创公司往往难以脱颖而出。这也许并不奇怪。今年的项目收到的申请几乎是之前Accel Atoms团队的四倍，其中有许多是首次创业者。印度不断发展的人工智能生态系统仍然主要集中在企业应用程序上，Swaroop表示，这些应用程序反映了这一点。大约62%的提交集中在生产力工具上，另外13%集中在软件开发和编码上，这意味着大约四分之三的应用程序是企业软件创意，而不是消费品。（Swaroop曾希望看到更多关于医疗保健和教育的想法。）谷歌人工智能期货基金的联合创始人兼董事乔纳森·西尔伯表示，选定的五家初创公司与谷歌预计人工智能将在现实世界中得到更深入应用的领域密切相关。Silber说，该计划并不要求初创公司完全使用谷歌的模型，并指出许多公司根据工作流程组合了多种模型。他说，目标是收集初创公司对谷歌模型在现实世界应用中的表现的反馈。然后，这些初创公司的见解可以反馈给谷歌DeepMind团队，以帮助改进未来的模型，在初创公司实验和人工智能开发之间创造Silber所说的“飞轮”。他告诉TechCrunch：“如果一家公司正在使用另一种模式，这意味着谷歌需要努力打造市场上最好的模式。”。Jagmeet为TechCrunch报道了初创公司、技术政策相关更新以及印度所有其他以技术为中心的重大发展。他曾担任NDTV的首席记者。您可以通过电子邮件联系或验证Jagmeet的外联活动mail@journalistjagmeet.com .
