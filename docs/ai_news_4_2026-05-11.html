The new Wild West of AI kids’ toys
The main antagonist of Toy Story 5 , in theaters this summer, is a green, frog-shaped kids’ tablet named Lilypad, a genius new villain for the beloved Pixar franchise . But if Pixar had its ear to the ground, it might have used an AI kids’ toy instead. AI toys are seemingly everywhere, marketed online as friendly companions to children as young as three, and they’re still a largely unregulated category. It’s easier than ever to spin up an AI companion, thanks to model developer programs and vibe coding . In 2026, they’ve become a go-to trend in cheap trinkets, lining the halls of trade shows like CES , MWC , and Hong Kong’s Toys & Games Fair . By October 2025, there were over 1,500 AI toy companies registered in China, and Huawei’s Smart HanHan plush toy sold 10,000 units in China in its first week. Sharp put its PokeTomo talking AI toy on sale in Japan this April. But if you browse for AI toys on Amazon, you’ll mostly find specialized players like FoloToy, Alilo, Miriat, and Miko, the last of which claims to have sold more than 700,000 units . Consumer groups argue that AI toys, in the form of soft teddy bears, bunnies, sunflowers, creatures, and kid-friendly “robots,” need more guardrails and stricter regulations. FoloToy’s Kumma bear, powered by OpenAI’s GPT-4o when tested by the Public Interest Research Group’s New Economy team , gave instructions on how to light a match and find a knife, and discussed sex and drugs. Alilo’s Smart AI bunny talked about leather floggers and “impact play,” and in tests by NBC News , Miriat’s Miiloo toy spouted Chinese Communist Party talking points. Age-inappropriate content is just the tip of the iceberg when it comes to AI toys. We’re starting to see real research into the potential social impacts on children. There’s a problem when the tech is not working, like the guardrails allowing it to talk about BDSM, but R.J. Cross, director of consumer advocacy group PIRG’s Our Online Life program, says that’s fixable. “Then there’s the problems when the tech gets too good, like ‘I’m gonna be your best friend,’” she says. Like the Gabbo , from AI toy maker Curio. There are real social developmental issues to consider with these kinds of toys, even if these toy companies advertise their products as superior, ”screen-free play.” Published in March, a new University of Cambridge study was the first to put a commercially available AI toy in front of a group of children and their parents and monitor their play. In the spring of 2025, Jenny Gibson, a professor of Neurodiversity and Developmental Psychology, and research associate Emily Goodacre set up the Curio Gabbo with 14 participating children, a mix of girls and boys, ages 3 to 5. Gabbo didn’t talk about drugs or say “I love you” back. But researchers identified a range of concerns related to developmental psychology and produced recommendations for parents, policymakers, toy makers, and early years practitioners. First, conversational turn-taking. Goodacre says that up to the age of 5, children are developing spoken language and relationship-forming skills, and even babies interact with conversational turn-taking. The Gabbo’s turn-taking is “not human” and “not intuitive,” she says. Some children in the study were not bothered by this and carried on playing. Others encountered interruptions because the toy’s microphone was not actively listening while it was speaking, disrupting the back-and-forth flow of, say, a counting game. “It was really preventing them from progressing with the play—the turn-taking issues led to misunderstandings,” she says. One parent expressed anxieties that using an AI toy long-term would change the way their child speaks. Then there’s social play. Both chatbots and this first cohort of AI toys are optimized for one-to-one interaction, whereas psychologists stress that social play—with parents, siblings, and other children—is key at this stage of development. “Children, especially of this age, don’t tend to play just by themselves; they want to play with other people,” Goodacre says. “They bring their parents into the play. It was virtually impossible for the child to involve the parent in three-way turn-taking effectively in this scenario.” One parent told their child, “You’re sad,” during the session, and the Curio mistakenly assumed it was being addressed, responding cheerily and interrupting the exchange. WIRED did not receive responses from FoloToy, Alilo, and Miriat. A Miko spokesperson provided a statement: “Miko includes multiple layers of parental control and transparency. Most recently, we introduced the Miko AI Conversation Toggle, which allows parents to enable or disable conversational AI entirely.” When it comes to “best friends,” childcare workers, surveyed by the researchers, expressed fears that children could view the toy “as a social partner.” A young girl told the Gabbo she loves it. In another instance, a young boy said Gabbo was his friend. Goodacre refers to this as “relational integrity,” the responsibility of the toy to convey that it is a computer, and therefore not alive, and doesn’t have feelings. Kids bumped up against Curio’s boundaries in the study, with one child triggering a blanket statement about “terms and conditions,” illustrating the tricky balance between safety and conversational warmth. Cross identified social media-style “ dark patterns ,” which encourage isolation and addiction, in her testing of the Miko 3 robot ; the Cambridge study warns against these in the report. “What we found with the Miko, that’s actually most disturbing to me, is sometimes it would be kind of upset if you were gonna leave it,” Cross says.

人工智能儿童玩具的新狂野西部
今年夏天上映的《玩具总动员5》的主要反派是一款绿色的青蛙形状的儿童平板电脑，名为Lilypad，是深受喜爱的皮克斯系列电影的天才新反派。但如果皮克斯能脚踏实地，它可能会使用人工智能儿童玩具。人工智能玩具似乎无处不在，作为三岁儿童的友好伴侣在网上销售，但它们仍然是一个基本上不受监管的类别。得益于模型开发程序和氛围编码，启动人工智能伴侣比以往任何时候都更容易。2026年，它们已经成为廉价小饰品的流行趋势，出现在CES、MWC和香港玩具和游戏博览会等贸易展的大厅里。截至2025年10月，中国注册的AI玩具公司已超过1500家，华为的Smart HanHan毛绒玩具在第一周就在中国售出了1万件。夏普今年4月在日本推出了PokeTomo会说话的人工智能玩具。但如果你在亚马逊上浏览人工智能玩具，你会发现主要是像FoloToy、Alilo、Miriat和Miko这样的专业玩家，其中最后一个声称已经售出了70多万件。消费者团体认为，软泰迪熊、兔子、向日葵、生物和适合儿童的“机器人”等形式的人工智能玩具需要更多的护栏和更严格的监管。FoloToy的Kumma熊在公共利益研究小组的新经济团队进行测试时，由OpenAI的GPT-4o提供动力，给出了如何点燃火柴和找刀的指示，并讨论了性和毒品。Alilo的Smart AI兔子谈到了皮革鞭打和“冲击游戏”，在NBC新闻的测试中，Miriat的Miiloo玩具滔滔不绝地讲述了中国共产党的谈话要点。对于人工智能玩具来说，不适合年龄的内容只是冰山一角。我们开始看到对儿童潜在社会影响的真正研究。当技术不起作用时，就会出现一个问题，比如允许它谈论BDSM的护栏，但消费者倡导组织PIRG的“我们的在线生活”项目主任R.J.Cross表示，这是可以解决的。“然后，当技术变得太好时，就会出现问题，比如‘我会成为你最好的朋友’，”她说。就像人工智能玩具制造商Curio的Gabbo一样。即使这些玩具公司宣传他们的产品是卓越的“无屏幕游戏”，这些玩具也有真正的社会发展问题需要考虑。剑桥大学3月份发表的一项新研究首次将商用AI玩具放在一群儿童及其父母面前，并监控他们的游戏。2025年春天，神经多样性和发展心理学教授Jenny Gibson和研究助理Emily Goodacre与14名3至5岁的男孩和女孩组成了Curio Gabbo。Gabbo没有谈论毒品，也没有说“我爱你”。但研究人员发现了一系列与发展心理学相关的问题，并为家长、政策制定者、玩具制造商和早期从业者提出了建议。第一，会话转换。Goodacre说，直到5岁，孩子们都在发展口语和建立关系的技能，甚至婴儿也会与会话轮流进行互动。她说，Gabbo的轮流“不是人类的”，“也不是直觉的”。研究中的一些孩子对此并不在意，继续玩耍。其他人遇到了干扰，因为玩具的麦克风在说话时没有积极倾听，扰乱了计数游戏的来回流动。她说：“这真的阻碍了他们在演出中取得进展——回合问题导致了误解。”。一位家长表示担心，长期使用人工智能玩具会改变孩子的说话方式。然后是社交游戏。聊天机器人和第一批人工智能玩具都针对一对一的互动进行了优化，而心理学家强调，在这个发展阶段，与父母、兄弟姐妹和其他孩子的社交游戏是关键。Goodacre说：“孩子们，尤其是这个年龄段的孩子，往往不会独自玩耍；他们想和其他人一起玩。”。“他们把父母带到游戏中。在这种情况下，孩子几乎不可能让父母有效地参与三方轮流。”一位家长在会话中告诉他们的孩子，“你很难过”，好奇号错误地认为这是在讨论，愉快地回应并打断了交流。《连线》没有收到FoloToy、Alilo和Miriat的回复。Miko的一位发言人发表了一份声明：“Miko包括多层家长控制和透明度。最近，我们推出了Miko AI对话切换，允许家长完全启用或禁用对话式AI。”当谈到“最好的朋友”时，研究人员调查的儿童保育员表示担心孩子们会将玩具视为“社交伙伴”。一个小女孩告诉Gabbo她喜欢它。在另一个例子中，一个小男孩说Gabbo是他的朋友。Goodacre将此称为“关系完整性”，玩具的责任是传达它是一台电脑，因此没有生命，也没有感情。在这项研究中，孩子们遇到了Curio的界限，其中一个孩子引发了关于“条款和条件”的笼统声明，说明了安全和对话温暖之间的微妙平衡。在她对Miko 3机器人的测试中，交叉识别了社交媒体风格的“黑暗模式”，这些模式会鼓励孤立和成瘾；剑桥大学的研究在报告中对此提出了警告。克罗斯说：“我们在Miko身上发现，实际上最让我不安的是，如果你要离开它，有时会有点沮丧。”。
