Perplexity's "Personal Computer" brings its AI agents to the, uh, Personal Computer
Last month Perplexity announced the confusingly named “Computer,” its cloud-based agent tool for completing tasks using a harness that makes use of multiple different AI models. This week, the company is moving that kind of functionality to the desktop with the confusingly named “ Personal Computer ,” now available in early access by invite only. Much like the cloud-based version, Personal Computer asks users to describe general objectives rather than specific computing tasks— an introductory video shows Personal Computer’s questions in a sidebar asking things like, “Create an interactive educational guide” and “create a podcast about whales.” But Personal Computer, running on a Mac Mini, also gives Perplexity’s agents local access to your files and apps, which it can open and manipulate directly to attempt to complete those tasks. That should sound familiar to users of the open source OpenClaw (previously Moltbot), which similarly allows users to let AI agents loose on their personal machines. From the outside, Personal Computer looks like a more buttoned-up, user-friendly version of the same concept, with an easy-to-read, dockable interface that can help users track multiple tasks. Perplexity users can also log in remotely to their local copy of Personal Computer, making it “controllable from any device, anywhere,” Perplexity says. If the prospect of letting “a persistent, digital proxy of you” loose on your private local files is worrying to you, you’re not alone. Perplexity promises that Personal Computer operates in a “secure environment with clear safeguards,” that all “sensitive actions” require user approval, that it keeps a “full audit trail” of every session, and that the system has a “kill switch” to stop things from getting out of hand in the most extreme cases. Those are likely to be necessary safeguards, given the surprisingly widespread stories of OpenClaw and other AI tools causing (or nearly causing) irreparable damage when given similar access. Without direct “early access” to Personal Computer, it’s hard to know just how well Perplexity’s offering moves this kind of agentic AI usage into something casual users can rely on in an everyday context on their local computer. But Perplexity isn’t alone in chasing that end goal; Nvidia is reportedly working on its own open source OpenClaw competitor , and there are plenty of startups and established brands setting up their own agentic AI tools with various levels of functionality. Aside from Personal Computer, Perplexity also announced an Enterprise version of the cloud-based Computer agent, which can interact with various corporate apps through connectors. The company’s Search , Agent , Embeddings , and Sandbox platforms are also now available via API rather than through Perplexity’s standard interface.

困惑的“个人电脑”将其人工智能代理带到了个人电脑上
上个月，Perplexity宣布了名为“Computer”的令人困惑的产品，这是其基于云的代理工具，用于使用利用多种不同AI模型的工具来完成任务。本周，该公司将这种功能转移到台式机上，推出了一款名为“个人电脑”的产品，该产品现在只能通过邀请的方式提前访问。与基于云的版本非常相似，个人电脑要求用户描述总体目标，而不是具体的计算任务——一段介绍性视频在侧边栏中显示了个人电脑的问题，比如“创建交互式教育指南”和“创建关于鲸鱼的播客”。但是，在Mac Mini上运行的个人电脑也让困惑的代理可以本地访问您的文件和应用程序，它可以直接打开和操作这些文件和应用以尝试完成这些任务。对于开源OpenClaw（以前称为Moltbot）的用户来说，这听起来应该很熟悉，它同样允许用户让人工智能代理在他们的个人机器上自由活动。从外观上看，个人电脑看起来像是一个更保守、用户友好的版本，具有易于阅读、可停靠的界面，可以帮助用户跟踪多个任务。困惑用户还可以远程登录到他们的本地个人电脑副本，使其“可以从任何设备、任何地方控制”，困惑说。如果你担心“你的持久数字代理”会泄露到你的私人本地文件中，那么你并不孤单。困惑保证个人电脑在“有明确保护措施的安全环境”中运行，所有“敏感操作”都需要用户批准，它对每个会话都有“完整的审计跟踪”，系统有一个“终止开关”，可以在最极端的情况下防止事情失控。鉴于OpenClaw和其他人工智能工具在获得类似访问权限时造成（或几乎造成）不可挽回的损害，这些可能是必要的保障措施。如果没有对个人电脑的直接“早期访问”，很难知道Perplexity的产品在多大程度上将这种代理人工智能的使用转化为普通用户在日常环境中可以在本地计算机上依赖的东西。但困惑并不是唯一一个追求最终目标的人；据报道，Nvidia正在开发自己的开源OpenClaw竞争对手，有很多初创公司和知名品牌正在建立自己的具有不同功能级别的代理AI工具。除了个人电脑，Perplexity还宣布了基于云的计算机代理的企业版，该代理可以通过连接器与各种企业应用程序进行交互。该公司的搜索、代理、嵌入和沙盒平台现在也可以通过API获得，而不是通过Perplexity的标准界面。
