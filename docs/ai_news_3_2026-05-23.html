AI is being used to resurrect the voices of dead pilots
In the latest sign of these AI-heavy times, the National Transportation Safety Board temporarily removed access to its docket system after discovering that voices of pilots who were killed in a UPS plane crash last year had been re-created using AI and were circulating on the internet. The NTSB is prohibited by federal law from including cockpit audio recordings in its docket system, which otherwise contains troves of data on investigations and has historically been open to the public. But the accident docket for this flight included a spectrogram file of the voice recorder. A spectrogram uses a mathematical process to turn sound signals, including low and high frequencies, into an image. Scott Manley, a popular YouTuber whose channel combines physics, astronomy, and video games, noted on X that it could be possible to reconstruct audio from the megabytes of data encoded in that image. And that’s what happened. People took the spectrogram, along with the publicly available transcript, to create approximations of the cockpit voice recorder audio from UPS Flight 2976 in Louisville, Kentucky, according to the NTSB . They used AI tools like Codex, according to posts on social media. The agency restored public access to the docket system on Friday but kept 42 investigations closed pending review — including the one related to Flight 2976.

人工智能正被用来复活死去飞行员的声音
在人工智能盛行的最新迹象中，美国国家运输安全委员会在发现去年在UPS飞机失事中遇难的飞行员的声音是使用人工智能重新创建的，并在互联网上传播后，暂时删除了对其备审案件系统的访问。联邦法律禁止NTSB将驾驶舱录音纳入其备审案件系统，否则该系统将包含大量调查数据，并且历来对公众开放。但这次飞行的事故记录包括录音机的频谱图文件。频谱图使用数学过程将声音信号（包括低频和高频）转换为图像。Scott Manley是一位受欢迎的YouTube用户，他的频道结合了物理学、天文学和电子游戏，他在X上指出，可以从该图像中编码的兆字节数据中重建音频。事情就是这样。据美国国家运输安全委员会称，人们利用频谱图和公开的文字记录，制作了肯塔基州路易斯维尔UPS 2976航班驾驶舱录音机音频的近似值。根据社交媒体上的帖子，他们使用了Codex等人工智能工具。该机构周五恢复了公众对备审案件系统的访问，但仍关闭了42项调查，等待审查，其中包括与2976航班有关的调查。
