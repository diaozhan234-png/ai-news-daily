South Korea to spend $1T on more memory chip production and humanoid robots
South Korea’s government and top tech companies are committing $1 trillion to several flagship megaprojects that could bolster global memory chip supply, build new AI data centers and spur commercial deployment of humanoid robots by 2028. The announcement comes as South Korean companies such as Samsung and SK Hynix have enjoyed record profits and stock valuations due to the AI industry’s demand for memory chips—with the subsequent supply strain leading to memory chip shortages and higher prices for consumer electronics. Meanwhile, Hyundai Motor Company is racing to mass manufacture humanoid robots developed by its subsidiary, Boston Dynamics, so that the robotic workers can start taking over certain laborious tasks in automotive factories and other workplaces. “We must secure the core elements of AI faster than any other country,” said South Korean President Lee Jae Myung in a televised speech on June 29, as reported by BBC News and other media outlets. “Semiconductors, physical AI, and AI data centers are the triple axis for a great leap forward.” But the initiatives also coincide with public debates about South Korean chipmakers’ huge profits during the AI boom and even policymaker proposals to distribute the excess wealth, along with South Korean labor unions pushing back against the prospect of humanoid robots entering the workforce. The most costly of the megaprojects involves Samsung and SK Hynix committing $585 billion to building new chip fabrication plants in the southwest provinces of South Korea, along with boosting semiconductor fab construction in the Seoul capital region, according to Reuters . The government’s goal is to double South Korea’s production of dynamic random-access memory (DRAM) within five years. However, the new fabs in South Korea’s southwestern region may need more time to get up and running, with SK Hynix Chairman Chey Tae-won commenting that it took nine years for the company to build a cluster of chip manufacturing facilities in Yongjin within the Seoul metropolitan area. So it’s unclear how soon global consumers can expect relief from sky-high memory chip prices and elevated prices for Apple’s Macs and Valve’s Steam Machine —especially if the AI boom continues and tech companies continue to buy up memory for AI data centers . The second flagship megaproject involves a $357 billion investment by the South Korean tech companies SK Group, GS Group, and Naver into building large-scale AI data centers in more outlying provinces, including South Chungcheong Province in the west, Gangwon Province in the east, and the North and South Jeolla Provinces in the southwest corner of South Korea. However, the new semiconductor chip fabs and the AI data centers require substantial electricity and water to operate. South Korea’s Ministry of Climate, Energy and Environment said it was working to secure 6.3 gigawatts of electricity and 650,000 tons of water for the southwestern chip plants, along with an additional 8 gigawatts of power to support the new AI data centers, according to The Korea Times . Renewable power and nuclear power plants would help supply the electricity needed for chip fabs and AI data centers, alongside fossil fuels, government officials said. Nuclear power and coal power both accounted for more than 30 percent of South Korea’s electricity generation in 2024, but the country’s reliance on natural gas for nearly 25 percent of electricity generation has left it vulnerable to supply shortages and surging prices during the ongoing Strait of Hormuz crisis. The third flagship megaproject revolves around the South Korean government assigning a “national strategic industry” designation to physical AI—the AI systems that enable robots and self-driving vehicles to interact more autonomously with the real world. The government aims to develop a Korean “general-purpose foundation model” based on a world model to support robots within three years, according to The Chosun Daily . Hyundai Motor Company has also committed $5.8 billion to build a robot manufacturing facility and AI data center in the Saemangeum region of North Jeolla Province in the southwest, The Chosun Daily reported. The South Korean automaker has already been helping Boston Dynamics —the US robotics company it acquired in 2021—use the South Korean supply chain in scaling up manufacturing to produce 30,000 Atlas humanoid robots each year by 2028. Similarly, the South Korean government announced it would aim to commercialize humanoid robots in 10 major industries by 2028, along with training 10,000 human workers as “AI robotics specialists” over the next five years, Reuters reported. However, South Korean workers are not feeling so optimistic about the prospect of competing with more robots. On June 25, Hyundai Motor’s labor union overwhelmingly approved a potential strike as it negotiated with the South Korean automaker about profit-sharing and job protections to offset the company’s planned deployment of Atlas humanoid robots, according to The Korea Times . A state labor mediation committee also granted the union the legal right to strike after suspending the arbitration process, with Hyundai Motor appealing to the union to return to the negotiating table. Other societal tensions have already arisen around South Korean chipmakers’ burgeoning profits from the AI boom. South Korean government officials have encouraged tech companies to share some of their unprecedented profits with their workers and smaller supplier companies. In May, the South Korean presidential chief of staff for policy even offhandedly proposed a “national dividend” for citizens based on excess tax revenue from South Korean’s companies’ AI-driven profits—though the government later described that as a personal view rather than an official proposal.

韩国将斥资1万亿美元增加内存芯片生产和人形机器人
韩国政府和顶级科技公司正在向几个旗舰大型项目投入1万亿美元，这些项目可以在2028年前增加全球存储芯片供应，建立新的人工智能数据中心，并刺激人形机器人的商业部署。这一消息发布之际，由于人工智能行业对存储芯片的需求，三星和SK海力士等韩国公司获得了创纪录的利润和股票估值，随后的供应紧张导致存储芯片短缺和消费电子产品价格上涨。与此同时，现代汽车公司正在竞相大规模生产其子公司波士顿动力公司开发的人形机器人，这样机器人工人就可以开始在汽车工厂和其他工作场所接管某些繁重的任务。据英国广播公司新闻和其他媒体报道，韩国总统李明博在6月29日的电视讲话中表示：“我们必须比任何其他国家更快地确保人工智能的核心要素。”。“半导体、物理人工智能和人工智能数据中心是实现跨越式发展的三重轴心。”但这些举措也与公众对韩国芯片制造商在人工智能繁荣时期的巨额利润的辩论相吻合，甚至与政策制定者分配多余财富的提议相吻合，同时韩国工会也反对人形机器人进入劳动力市场的前景。据路透社报道，成本最高的大型项目包括三星和SK海力士承诺投入5850亿美元在韩国西南部省份建设新的芯片制造厂，同时加强首尔首都地区的半导体晶圆厂建设。政府的目标是在五年内将韩国的动态随机存取存储器（DRAM）产量翻一番。然而，韩国西南部地区的新晶圆厂可能需要更多时间来启动和运行，SK海力士董事长Chey Tae-won评论说，该公司花了九年时间在首尔大都市区的Yongjin建立了一个芯片制造工厂集群。因此，目前尚不清楚全球消费者多久能从极高的存储芯片价格以及苹果Mac和Valve Steam Machine的价格上涨中得到缓解，尤其是如果人工智能热潮持续下去，科技公司继续为人工智能数据中心购买内存的话。第二个旗舰大型项目涉及韩国科技公司SK Group、GS Group和Naver投资3570亿美元，在更偏远的省份建设大型人工智能数据中心，包括西部的忠清南道、东部的江原道以及韩国西南角的全罗北道和南道。然而，新的半导体芯片制造厂和人工智能数据中心需要大量的电力和水来运行。据《韩国时报》报道，韩国气候、能源和环境部表示，正在努力为西南部的芯片厂提供6.3吉瓦的电力和65万吨的水，并额外提供8吉瓦的电源来支持新的人工智能数据中心。政府官员表示，可再生能源和核电站将有助于供应芯片制造厂和人工智能数据中心所需的电力，以及化石燃料。2024年，核电和煤电均占韩国发电量的30%以上，但该国近25%的发电量依赖天然气，这使其在霍尔木兹海峡危机期间容易受到供应短缺和价格飙升的影响。第三个旗舰大型项目围绕着韩国政府将物理人工智能指定为“国家战略产业”，即使机器人和自动驾驶汽车能够更自主地与现实世界互动的人工智能系统。据《朝鲜日报》报道，韩国政府计划在三年内开发一种基于世界模型的韩国“通用基础模型”，以支持机器人。据《朝鲜日报》报道，现代汽车公司还承诺投资58亿美元，在西南部全罗北道的新万根地区建造一个机器人制造工厂和人工智能数据中心。这家韩国汽车制造商已经在帮助其于2021年收购的美国机器人公司波士顿动力公司利用韩国供应链扩大制造规模，到2028年每年生产3万台阿特拉斯人形机器人。同样，据路透社报道，韩国政府宣布，到2028年，将在10个主要行业实现人形机器人的商业化，并在未来五年内培训1万名人类工人成为“人工智能机器人专家”。然而，韩国工人对与更多机器人竞争的前景并不乐观。据《韩国时报》报道，6月25日，现代汽车工会压倒性地批准了一项潜在的罢工，因为它与这家韩国汽车制造商就利润分享和工作保护进行了谈判，以抵消该公司计划部署的Atlas人形机器人。在暂停仲裁程序后，州劳动调解委员会还授予工会罢工的合法权利，现代汽车呼吁工会重返谈判桌。围绕韩国芯片制造商从人工智能热潮中迅速增长的利润，其他社会紧张局势已经出现。韩国政府官员鼓励科技公司与工人和较小的供应商公司分享一些前所未有的利润。今年5月，韩国总统政策办公室主任甚至轻率地提出了一项基于韩国公司人工智能驱动利润的超额税收的公民“国家红利”，尽管政府后来将其描述为个人观点，而不是官方提议。
